about us

Imagine Dragons is a band born to tell stories through music. Our sound blends alternative rock, pop, and electronic elements, creating intense and emotional songs that connect with millions of people around the world.

From the beginning, we have sought to do more than just make music: we want to convey energy, hope, and authenticity in every song.

Our story began in Las Vegas in 2008. We started playing in small venues and local events, building our sound step by step.

Everything changed when we released our first album, Night Visions, in 2012. Its success took us to international stages and marked the beginning of a career full of musical evolution.

Over the years, we have continued to grow and experiment with new styles, always maintaining our essence.

Dan Reynolds main singer

I am the lead singer and one of the main songwriters of the band. My voice is the way I share emotions, personal struggles, and moments of hope with our audience.

Many of our songs come from personal experiences, and I try to transform those feelings into lyrics that people can relate to. Music, for me, has always been a way to communicate honestly and connect with others through shared emotions.

When I perform on stage, I try to give everything I have. The energy from the audience becomes part of the performance and makes every concert unique.

Singing live allows me to feel that connection directly, turning each show into something special for both us and the fans.

Outside the stage, I spend a lot of time working on lyrics and melodies, always searching for new ways to express ideas and emotions.

Writing music is a very personal process, but it becomes even more meaningful when those songs reach people and become part of their lives.

Ben McKee bassist

I play the bass in the band, and my job is to provide the rhythmic and harmonic foundation that supports our music. The bass connects the rhythm and the melody, helping the songs feel strong and balanced .

Even though it often stays in the background, it plays a key role in creating the groove and energy of each track.

I enjoy creating bass lines that work closely with the drums to build a solid rhythm section. When the bass and drums are locked together, the whole song feels more powerful and dynamic. That connection is essential to the overall sound of the band.

Being part of this group means constantly collaborating and sharing ideas. Each song is built step by step , with everyone contributing their own perspective.

For me, the most rewarding part is seeing how those ideas come together to create music that people around the world can enjoy .

Wayne Sermon guitarist

I am the guitarist of the band, and my role is to help shape the musical atmosphere of our songs . Through riffs, melodies, and different guitar sounds, I contribute to creating the style that defines our music.

I enjoy experimenting with different tones and techniques so that each track can have its own character and identity .

For me, the guitar is not only about playing strong riffs but also about building textures and layers that complement the rest of the band .

Sometimes that means creating powerful moments , and other times it means adding subtle details that give depth to a song.

Working together with the other members of the band is an important part of the creative process.

We share ideas, experiment with different sounds, and gradually shape each song until it feels complete. That collaboration is what allows our music to continue evolving over time.

Daniel Platzman drummer

I am the drummer of the band, and my responsibility is to create the rhythm and energy that drive our music forward. The drums help shape the dynamics of a song, from quiet and emotional moments to powerful and explosive sections.

I enjoy finding rhythms that match the mood and message of each track.

Playing the drums is about more than keeping time. It’s about creating movement and intensity that support the entire band. Every beat, fill, or change in rhythm can influence how a song feels and how the audience experiences it.

Performing live is one of the most exciting parts of being a drummer. The energy of the crowd and the atmosphere of the concert make every performance different.

In those moments, the rhythm becomes a shared experience between the band and the audience.
